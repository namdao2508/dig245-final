FAQ

How do I invest?

Start by reading the news catalog which will provide you with key information about the projections of certain stocks and ETFs. Then you can click on the trade button of the stock/ETF of your choice to either buy or sell the equity.

What are stocks?

Stocks, often referred to as shares or equity, represent ownership in a company. When you buy a stock, you become a shareholder, owning a small part of that company. The value of stocks fluctuates based on the company's performance and market conditions. If the company does well, the value of your stocks may increase. Alternative, if the company does poorly, the value of your stocks may decrease. Investing in stocks comes with risks, but it also offers the opportunity for significant financial gains.

What are Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs)?

Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs) are investment funds that are traded on stock exchanges, much like individual stocks. An ETF holds assets such as stocks, bonds, or commodities, and it typically tracks an index, a sector, a commodity, or other assets. One of the main advantages of ETFs is that they offer diversification. Since an ETF can hold hundreds or thousands of stocks across various industries, or it can be focused on a specific sector or asset class, it allows investors to reduce the risk that comes with investing in individual stocks. This makes them an attractive option for both beginner and experienced investors looking to build a diversified portfolio.

How do I identify possible catalysts?

Identifying possible catalysts in the context of investments means spotting events or developments that could significantly impact the price of a stock, a sector, or the market as a whole. Here are some key strategies to identify such catalysts: Earnings Reports, Economic Indicators, Industry Developments, Mergers and Acquisitions, Geopoliticial Events, Company-Specific News, Market Sentiment, Technological Breakthroughs, etc...
